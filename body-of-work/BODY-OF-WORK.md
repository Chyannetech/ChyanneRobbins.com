# Body of Work

## Field of Study

This body of work studies the conditions that shape how people think, feel, decide, and act.

Conditions may include environments, technology, interfaces, products, physical spaces, conversations, rituals, incentives, information, systems, culture, and the countless invisible factors that influence human experience.

The work is not centered on any one medium.

It is centered on understanding the conditions that shape people, wherever those conditions exist.

---

# Why This Body of Work Exists

This body of work exists to develop understanding, not simply publish content.

Its purpose is to investigate observations, uncover principles, and explore how those principles might inform better experiences, technology, products, organizations, and everyday life.

The goal is not to collect ideas.

The goal is to build a coherent body of work that becomes more useful over time.

---

# The Knowledge Pipeline

Every idea moves through the same progression.

Experience

↓

Observation

↓

Understanding

↓

Implication

↓

Experimentation

↓

Practice

Not every idea will move through every stage.

Some observations remain observations.

Others eventually become products, technologies, experiences, or professional work.

The value of the body of work comes from allowing ideas to mature over time rather than forcing them toward predetermined outcomes.

---

# Stage One — Experience

Question:

> What happened?

Experiences are the raw material of the work.

They may come from travel, products, software, books, conversations, public spaces, research papers, museums, technology, organizations, everyday life, or simple curiosity.

An experience is not valuable because it is extraordinary.

It is valuable because it creates an opportunity to notice something.

---

# Stage Two — Observation (Journal)

Question:

> What did I notice that I hadn't noticed before?

Journal exists to capture shifts in perception.

It documents observations rather than conclusions.

The purpose of Journal is not to teach, persuade, or solve.

Its purpose is to preserve the moment where understanding begins.

Strong Journal entries often end with curiosity instead of certainty.

A Journal entry does not need to resolve anything. Its job is to discover the right question — the one a future Research investigation can go on to explore.

Journal preserves the moment an observation becomes a question. Research is where that question gets investigated.

*(Added 2026-07-08, once publishing the first two Journal entries — Austin and Touch Grass — made this relationship visible in hindsight rather than something planned in advance. Both entries ended on a question they didn't answer; naming that pattern here makes it a deliberate principle instead of a coincidence.)*

---

# Stage Three — Understanding (Research)

Question:

> What larger principle helps explain this observation?

Research expands an observation beyond its original context.

The experience becomes evidence—not the subject itself.

Existing research, behavioral science, design, psychology, public health, technology, history, or other disciplines deepen understanding of what has already been observed.

Research begins with observation, not literature.

Existing knowledge expands understanding.

It does not replace the act of noticing.

---

# Stage Four — Implication

Question:

> If this principle is true, what does it imply for the way we design products, technology, organizations, services, environments, or experiences?

This stage intentionally shifts from explanation toward design.

Rather than asking what happened, it asks what should be reconsidered.

Implication transforms understanding into possibility.

---

# Stage Five — Experimentation (Chyless World)

Question:

> How might this idea be tested?

Chyless World is the public laboratory for this body of work.

Chyless World is the current name for this stage, not its definition — the stage is the public laboratory itself; what it is called may change without changing what this stage means.

It exists to explore ideas through experiments.

Those experiments may take many forms, including prototypes, software, tools, publications, experiences, design concepts, creative projects, or other explorations.

The purpose of experimentation is not validation.

It is learning.

---

# Stage Six — Practice (Creative Studio)

Question:

> How can this understanding help solve meaningful real-world problems?

The Creative Studio is the professional application of the body of work.

Creative Studio is likewise the current name for this stage, not its definition — the stage is the professional application itself, however it is branded.

Organizations do not hire research.

They hire better outcomes.

This work contributes by helping design better conditions that support those outcomes.

The research informs the practice.

The practice strengthens the research.

Each makes the other more valuable.

# Research Methodology

Stage Three establishes that research begins with observation, not literature. This section makes that principle operational — the order in which a Research investigation is actually conducted, arrived at through practice rather than decided in advance.

1. Research begins with observation, not theory.

2. Journal exists to preserve the observation and discover the right question.

3. Research begins by investigating that question, not by trying to prove a conclusion.

4. Existing literature is consulted as multiple explanatory lenses, not as authorities the investigation gets organized around.

5. Each framework is evaluated directly against the observation — what it explains well, what it explains only partially, and what remains unexplained.

6. Original frameworks are only developed if a genuine explanatory gap remains after that evaluation.

7. Any proposed framework should eventually be tested through Chyless World prototypes or experiments whenever possible.

8. New observations may refine or replace earlier frameworks over time.

> Observe first. Question honestly. Consult multiple lenses. Follow the evidence. Synthesize only when earned. Test through design. Observe again.

(Added 2026-07-08, formalized after the first Research investigation — into why the same environment produces different experiences for different people — worked through this exact sequence in practice before it was written down here.)

# Demonstration

Every published piece should quietly demonstrate a way of thinking that someone else could trust to be applied to a meaningful problem.

The publication does not exist to claim expertise.

It demonstrates expertise through careful observation, thoughtful reasoning, and consistent application.

---

# The Publishing Filter

Before beginning any Research article, ask:

1. What did I observe?

2. What enduring principle does this observation reveal?

3. What new way of seeing does this article offer?

4. If this principle is true, where else does it matter?

5. Who would benefit from understanding this principle?

6. How does this strengthen the overall body of work?

If these questions cannot be answered, the work probably is not ready to publish.

---

# The Body of Work Test

Before publishing, ask:

> If someone wanted to understand how I think, would this article help them?

The goal is not simply to communicate information.

The goal is to make a way of thinking visible.

---

# What We Do Not Do

This body of work is not built to:

- chase trends
- maximize engagement
- produce hot takes
- publish for consistency instead of curiosity
- summarize existing research without adding perspective
- separate research from real-world application
- publish Research that cannot eventually inform better products, technology, experiences, organizations, or decisions — Journal is exempt from this by design; its purpose is to preserve observation and curiosity, not to resolve them (see Stage Two)

The publication should always contribute something beyond what already exists.

It should leave the reader seeing something they had not seen before.

# A Living Body of Work

This document defines the philosophy behind this body of work.

It should change rarely.

The body of work itself should evolve continuously.

When new insights emerge, they should first challenge the work—not the framework.

The framework changes only when the work reveals that it no longer describes how we think.

Any change to this document should be dated and briefly explained in place, so the framework's own history stays visible rather than silently rewritten.
