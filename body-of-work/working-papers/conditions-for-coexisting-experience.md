# Working Paper — Conditions for Coexisting Experience

Internal document. Not for publication, not a draft of the Research article. Records what the investigation has actually learned so far, and how it got there, before any decision is made about the structure or claims of the published piece.

**Originating observation (Journal):** ["I Thought I Knew What Kind of Experience I Was Walking Into"](../../website/content/journal/the-experience-i-walked-into.md) — an outdoor community gathering, open invitation, produced visibly divergent behavior (solitary/restorative vs. social/high-energy) that stayed peacefully coexistent for the whole afternoon.

**Current research question:** What conditions allow one environment to support multiple, equally legitimate experiences without requiring them to converge into one?

**Working definition of experience:** the transactional relationship between a person and the conditions they encounter — co-constituted through engagement, not delivered by the environment or constructed solely inside the individual, unfolding before, during, and after. Adopted for fit to this body of work, not asserted as the settled account in a field where that's still contested (see §4 for its sourcing).

**Contrast cases completed:** Jane Jacobs' urban sociology (sidewalk vs. superblock), open-plan office research, the public library.

---

## 1. Conditions that now appear durable

### Structural permission (necessary, not explanatory)

Originated in the literature phase as the leading candidate, drawn from Barker's behavior-settings theory: a loosely programmed setting leaves room for divergence that a tightly scripted one wouldn't. At that point it was treated as close to the whole answer.

It didn't survive contact with the contrast cases as a driver. Jacobs' superblock plazas were nominally just as open and flexible as her successful sidewalks, and failed anyway. The open-plan office was the most structurally permissive environment tested — no walls at all — and produced the worst outcome. The library, the one environment that openly ranks and constrains behavior (quiet is the stated default), succeeded anyway.

**Status: durable, but demoted.** Permission remains a background condition — nothing coexists in a environment that actively forbids it — but it explains none of the variance between the cases. It was the wrong lever from the start of the contrast phase, which is itself the finding.

### Multiplicity of independent purpose

Not present anywhere in the original literature survey. Emerged specifically from the Jacobs case: her successful sidewalks work because different people pass through for reasons that don't depend on each other — a commuter, a shopper, someone sitting on a stoop. Her failed superblocks lacked exactly this; the plazas had no staggered anchor-purposes drawing people through, sat empty, and empty became unsafe rather than shared.

The library confirmed it independently — Klinenberg's point that libraries hold people together who differ in politics, interests, and reasons for being there, with no relationship to each other's purpose.

The office case initially looked like a complication: workers also arrive with different purposes (focus vs. collaboration), yet coexistence still failed. Resolving that required sharpening the condition rather than abandoning it: office purposes aren't actually independent in Jacobs' or Klinenberg's sense — they're usually tied to the same team, the same deliverable, the same employer's output. "Different" is not the same as "not mutually dependent on a shared outcome." Read that way, the office case sharpens the condition instead of contradicting it.

**Status: durable**, confirmed independently in two cases (Jacobs, library) and clarified rather than weakened by the third (office).

### Protected room per mode (revised from two earlier, narrower candidates)

The office case originally produced two separate candidates: (a) no imposed cost between modes — noise and presence shouldn't cross involuntarily from one mode into another; (b) no institutional ranking of one mode as more legitimate than another.

The library broke (b) as originally stated. Libraries rank openly — quiet is the stated default, loud is contained rather than treated as equally the norm — and coexistence still works. The candidate had to be revised, not discarded: what actually seems to matter isn't equal status, it's whether each mode gets protected room of its own — spatial zoning and an active steward in the library's case, self-selected distance and objects (a blanket, a book) at the gathering, staggered timing on Jacobs' sidewalks. Formal ranking is compatible with coexistence as long as the lower-ranked mode still has somewhere to exist without being pushed out entirely.

(a) survived and generalized cleanly under this revision: the office's flat, unzoned floor plan gave neither mode anywhere to go — a phone ringing at a vacant desk reaches every desk, unlike a lawn with room to spread out or a library with sound-absorbing walls between zones.

**Status: durable**, but explicitly logged as a revision, not a first-try success — see §2.

### Still open, not even tentatively confirmed

**Shared undertone** ("relaxed, but not still" — the ambient quality that ran through both clusters at the original gathering). No contrast case was designed to test for this, and none of the three produced evidence either for or against it. This is not "tentative" — it's untouched. Recorded here so it isn't quietly dropped.

**Active stewardship.** Present only in the library (librarians actively managing zone boundaries in real time). The gathering had none. Jacobs' sidewalks have something closer to diffuse, informal stewardship — "eyes on the street" spread across the whole population rather than a designated role — which may or may not count as the same thing. The office had none, and its absence plausibly contributed to the failure. One case is not enough to promote this from candidate to condition.

## 2. Assumptions revised or abandoned

- **"Loosely programmed = the enabling factor"** (Barker, literature phase) → revised into "permission is necessary but explains none of the variance," once the library succeeded despite being the least loosely programmed environment tested.
- **"No institutional ranking of modes"** (office case) → revised, not abandoned, into "protected room per mode" once the library's open ranking of quiet-over-loud still produced successful coexistence. The original candidate wasn't wrong so much as too narrow — a special case of the broader condition.
- **"Dual-dial setting"** (tone vs. activity as two separate axes of programming, raised during the literature-gap-analysis phase) → effectively retired. Flagged early as possibly describing the environment rather than the relationship, and no contrast case was ever built to test it directly. Recorded here as quietly dropped, not disproven — a working paper should show what fell away, not only what survived.
- **Parking-lot candidate "an environment's authorship is proportional to how tightly it's programmed"** → now effectively superseded. Program strength does not predict which cases succeeded.
- **Parking-lot candidate "the environment offers, the person completes"** (Gibson-flavored) → never tested against a contrast case directly. Still sitting in the parking lot, looking increasingly thin now that multiplicity-of-purpose and protected-room explain more of the variance than pure affordance-selection does on its own.
- **The definition of "experience" itself** moved twice, not once: from an unstated assumption (treating behavioral divergence as evidence of experiential divergence, never made explicit until the definitional pass) → to a data-constrained candidate, "the retrospectively appraised account" (rejected as too narrow — a definition shaped by what one journal entry gave us access to, not by where the body of work is heading) → to the adopted transactional definition. Logged here as its own revision, not only under the definition's own heading, per the instruction to preserve how assumptions actually changed.
- **Observer-dependent visibility** (the sixth component of the original phenomenon breakdown) was not revised or abandoned — it was deliberately set outside the framework as a methodological note about how the investigation itself came to notice the phenomenon, not a claim about the phenomenon. Worth keeping that distinction clean: some things get revised because they were wrong; this one was set aside because it was answering a different question.

## 3. What remains genuinely unexplained

**Likely reachable through more literature:**
- The shared-undertone question — untested by any contrast case, but plausibly addressable through literature on ambient affect, atmosphere, or soundscape research before it would require direct observation.
- Whether active stewardship is genuinely necessary in strong-norm environments or incidental to libraries specifically — answerable, in principle, by finding further contrast cases with strong norms and no steward (a self-organizing market, a shared trail) before assuming an experiment is required.

**Likely requires original experimentation (Chyless World), not more reading:**
- **Whether multiplicity of independent purpose and protected room are actually independent conditions, or whether one is downstream of the other.** In every case examined so far, both were present together (Jacobs, library) or both absent together (office). No case has ever isolated one from the other. Real-world contrast cases weren't built as experiments and may not offer one. This is the clearest, most specific candidate for a deliberately engineered prototype — vary one condition, hold the other constant, and see whether coexistence still holds.
- **The origin of differing purposes people arrive with.** Flagged as far back as the original A/B/C reorganization: every theory in Investigation A takes differing needs and goals as a given input, none explain where they came from. This increasingly looks like a fact about a specific population, not a general mechanism literature can resolve — a candidate for direct observation rather than further reading.
- **Whether the transactional definition of experience holds outside physical environments** — technology, AI companions, conversation. Deliberately deferred earlier as premature to chase before the physical-environment thread was developed. Still open, and still the eventual test of whether this definition was the right choice for the body of work as a whole, not just for this one investigation.

## 4. The theoretical lenses, evaluated one final time

**Contributed the most explanatory power:**
- **Jane Jacobs** — not part of the original literature set at all, and ended up supplying the single most load-bearing condition (multiplicity of independent purpose) almost entirely on its own, with a rare documented success/failure pair inside one body of work.
- **Dewey's transactional theory of experience** — did double duty. It didn't just supply the working definition of "experience"; it retroactively explains why protected room per mode matters at all — a transaction needs room to unfold on its own terms, which a definition of experience as a fixed internal state or a fixed environmental delivery wouldn't have predicted.
- **Self-Determination Theory** — the strongest single-theory fit for the original behavioral clustering at the gathering (autonomy-seeking vs. relatedness-seeking). It never re-entered the investigation once the conditions/contrast phase began — worth being honest that it explained the starting observation well without turning out to be load-bearing for the conditions question.

**Became supporting context rather than central:**
- **Barker (behavior settings / program strength)** — useful for framing the question that sent the investigation looking for a second condition in the first place; demoted once shown insufficient on its own.
- **Gibson (affordances)** — remains true as a mechanism of selection (what gets noticed and used), but never explained coexistence itself.
- **Third place theory (Oldenburg)** — directionally right (neutral ground, loose norms) but superseded by Jacobs and Klinenberg's more specific, evidence-backed versions of the same territory.
- **Goffman, expectation/Bayesian perception, construal level theory** — genuinely useful for Investigation A (what a person brings in), but didn't carry into the conditions/contrast phase. Still true; not currently load-bearing.
- **Kahneman, Lazarus, Pine & Gilmore, Husserl, UX (ISO 9241-210)** — did one specific job, defining what "experience" means for this investigation, and exited once that job was done. Worth recording plainly that they were a one-time definitional pass, not an ongoing explanatory lens, rather than implying they're still active in the analysis.

**Proved less relevant than expected:**
- **Salutogenesis / sense of coherence** — flagged as a weak fit from the earliest gap analysis (built for health outcomes under stress, not for a single afternoon's behavioral divergence) and never recovered relevance.
- **Collins' interaction ritual chains** — produced the sharpest early open question (why didn't the high-energy ritual absorb the quiet cluster the way the theory would predict), but the eventual answer came from Jacobs and the library, not from extending Collins' own framework. Collins named the puzzle. He didn't end up resolving it.

---

## 5. Is an original framework earned yet?

**No.**

Two conditions are now durable across independent, structurally different cases: multiplicity of independent purpose, and protected room per mode. That's real progress, not nothing. But the methodology's own bar (§6 of Research Methodology, in `BODY-OF-WORK.md`) is a genuine explanatory gap remaining after evaluating the literature and the evidence — not simply having completed enough contrast cases to feel ready.

The actual gap left is narrow and specific: whether the two durable conditions are independent of each other or whether one depends on the other. Every case examined moved them together. Publishing a framework now would mean either quietly asserting independence we've never actually observed, or dressing up "two conditions, confirmed together three times" in framework language it hasn't earned — which fails the test built into the question itself: does this differ from simply combining existing evidence, or not?

**What would actually earn it:** a case — real or deliberately engineered — where the two conditions come apart. That's the gate, not elapsed time or case count. If a future contrast case or a Chyless World prototype isolates them, and the result still explains something the individual conditions don't explain alone, that's when synthesis is earned. Until then, this stays two well-supported conditions and one clearly named open question, not a framework.
